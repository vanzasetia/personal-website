If you ever [play video games](/blog/video-game/), you may play as a character. You are the player who controls it. Then, you fight the enemies. Those enemies are Artificial Intelligence (AI). After that, who will win? You or AI?

When you play a game with multiple stages and you need to kill all the enemies to win each stage, you may fail many times before you can complete it. The good news is that in the end, **you can win.**

You can learn from the mistakes that you make. Then, you can create a better strategy to beat those enemies.

Now, if you use that perspective when facing ChatGPT (or any LLMs), who is ultimately better? **You!** You can be better at programming than ChatGPT. ChatGPT is only good at being average, *sometimes*. It is not specifically good for anything. It will lose against humans in the end—just like gamers beat their enemies in a game.

Another thing. Some online games allow you to practice with AI. You can think of it as a chance to improve. You can fight with them until you feel you are ready to fight with other players, humans.

If you use that mindset when using ChatGPT, you can use it to make yourself better. You can learn from it until you feel you are ready to contribute to society or to become a professional.

## What if ChatGPT is getting smarter?

A game has just gotten an update that makes the AI enemies smarter. Good gamers will be happy since the game becomes more challenging. They can learn new tricks or strategies to beat their enemies.

Now, if ChatGPT is getting smarter, you can choose to use it to be better. **Learn from it and then beat it!**
