Having a lightweight website is totally fine because you are kind to

- many people around the world who have either a slow or [no internet](https://worldpopulationreview.com/country-rankings/countries-without-internet); and
- millions of people who do not have [cheap internet](https://worldpopulationreview.com/country-rankings/internet-cost-by-country) or do not have unlimited internet or [both](https://web.archive.org/web/20240425065129/http://whatdoesmysitecost.com/); and
- many people who temporarily have no access to fast internet.

I am not saying that you should have a plain website. I suggest that you [*progressively* enhance](/blog/progressive-enhancement/) your website. This means when only plain HTML arrives, your website is still understandable. When only plain HTML and a stylesheet are loaded, your website is still accessible.

Use your creativity and logic to develop a website with progressive enhancement.

Remember that [your website](/blog/tips-web-performance/) can be [expensive for others](https://www.creativebloq.com/web-design/shocking-cost-visiting-website-31514449). Be nice.
